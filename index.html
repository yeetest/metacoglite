01 / Problem Statement

The "Last Mile" gap in AI-assisted learning

STUDENT
Copy-paste dependency
Students use raw AI as a shortcut — pasting questions, copying answers — without building the metacognitive habits that drive real score improvement.
TEACHER
Repetitive low-value work
80% of teacher prep time goes to explaining vocabulary, basic question types, and passage structure — tasks AI can handle — leaving no bandwidth for high-value coaching.
PARENT
Invisible progress
Parents bear high tutoring costs with no visibility into how their child is learning — only test scores, not process. No way to assess if AI is helping or enabling avoidance.
02 / Theoretical Framework

Three pillars of effective AI-assisted learning

01
Technical Affordance
RAG + Prompt Engineering
56 authentic TOEFL passages embedded in a RAG knowledge base. Chain-of-Thought, Few-Shot, and Step-by-Step prompting handle complex question types like Sentence Insertion and Inference.
02
Skilled Competence
Metacognitive Intervention
A 90-minute intervention course teaches students how to use the tool strategically. The AI shifts from answering to asking — initiating 30–70% of interactions using Reciprocal Teaching: Predict, Question, Clarify, Summarize.
03
Social Regulation
Nudge Theory + Default Design
Default options guide students toward deep processing. The AI uses "teachable agent" mode — surfacing its own limitations so students debug it, turning AI errors into critical thinking exercises.

03 / Product Demo

Watch the scaffolding in action

元
PREDICTING
Before we read the passage "Powering the Industrial Revolution," take a moment — what themes or ideas do you predict this text will cover? What do you already know about this topic?
S
I think it'll be about steam engines and coal... factories maybe?
元
Good — you've activated relevant schema. Now, as you read, track which of your predictions hold up and which don't. The mismatches are where the most interesting learning happens.

When you're ready with the first paragraph, what's the main claim the author is making?
Predicting activates prior knowledge and sets a purpose for reading
元
CLARIFYING
Which part of the passage are you finding difficult right now?
S
The phrase "made little sense" in paragraph 2 — I don't understand what "made little sense" in context means here.
元
Good catch — that's a subtle one. Before I explain, look at the sentence structure: what is the subject? What is "making little sense"? Try to identify what the author is saying was illogical.
S
Is it... the idea of owning more land than you could use?
元
Exactly. In nomadic societies, land accumulation was irrational — you couldn't carry it. The author is highlighting how agricultural logic was culturally foreign, not just unfamiliar. This is key to the passage's argument about social transformation.
Clarifying: AI guides the student to locate confusion before explaining
S
For the sentence insertion question, I think the answer is [B] because it sounds right after that sentence.
元
QUESTIONING
"Sounds right" is a feeling — let's make it a reason. Look at the sentence to be inserted: does it start with a pronoun or a transition word? If so, what does it refer back to?
S
It starts with "This"... so it must be referring to something mentioned just before it.
元
Right. Now scan positions [A], [B], [C], [D] — which position has a noun or concept in the preceding sentence that "This" could logically refer to? That's your answer, not intuition.
S
Oh — position [C] has "the new transportation system" right before it, and the inserted sentence says "This network..." so it must be [C].
元
Correct. And notice what just happened — you didn't need me to give you the answer. You applied a referent-tracking strategy. That strategy works on every sentence insertion question, not just this one.
Scaffolding: AI asks questions instead of answering them
04 / User Research

Data-driven from day one

76 In-service Teachers

Surveyed to understand which tasks teachers most wanted automated and which required human judgment. The findings directly shaped V2's feature set.

  • Vocabulary explanation ranked #1 most-wanted automated task
  • Content logic analysis rated most critical — cannot be delegated to AI
  • Teacher-initiated interactions: over 50% of classroom exchanges
  • Grammar explanation tested and abandoned — POS tagging error rate too high

10 Student Case Studies

Pre/post testing with behavioral coding. Student interaction logs coded into "processed" vs. "unprocessed" categories to measure metacognitive engagement.

  • Metacognitive strategy frequency correlated with score improvement (r = 0.994)
  • Only 1 of 5 students showed significant strategy change after Phase 1
  • Finding led to key pivot: embed strategies into tool design, not just instruction
  • Students who "debugged" AI errors showed highest critical thinking gains
05 / Product Iteration

Three data-driven iterations

VERSION 0
Trigger: Access barrier — domestic users couldn't reach GPT
Mirror Site Deployment
Built a mirror server to allow Chinese users to access the tool without VPN. Expanded the user base from 0 to a testable scale for Phase 1 research.
→ Outcome: Sufficient users to conduct pre/post behavioral study
VERSION 1
Trigger: Pre/post data showed metacognitive strategy use correlated with score gains
Metacognitive Intervention + Reciprocal Teaching Mode
Added a 90-minute intervention course. Redesigned AI interaction model: shifted from answering questions to asking them. Implemented Ann Brown's Reciprocal Teaching method as the core interaction protocol. AI now initiates 30–70% of exchanges.
→ Outcome: Increased autonomous practice frequency; metacognitive behaviors became observable and measurable
VERSION 2
Trigger: 76-teacher survey revealed vocabulary and grammar as top teacher needs
Vocabulary Module Launch + Grammar Module Abandoned
Added structured vocabulary explanation module with custom prompt engineering for format, etymology, and collocations. Tested grammar explanation in parallel. Grammar POS tagging error rate was unacceptably high — risked misguidance. Decision: ship vocabulary, kill grammar. Data, not assumption.
→ Outcome: Vocabulary explanations matched human teacher accuracy at 24/7 availability. Grammar removed before harming users.
